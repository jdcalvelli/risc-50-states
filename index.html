The Center for RISC researched and graded all 50 States to determine what they have done to promote data science education.

The US landscape is not impressive.

Most states have not even recognized the importance of Data Science in a student's education.

Those who have are just starting their processes of planning and implementing changes.

A
  • Incorporation of data science into math and other mandatory classes such as science and social studies.
  • Extensive, optional math courses focused on data science; flexible graduation requirements that make those courses attractive options.
  • Resources at state level dedicated to promoting and supporting data science instruction.
  • Data science education starts in middle school if not earlier.
B
  • Mandatory data analysis standards or units in math and ideally other classes.
  • Additional data science classes or units in optional classes.
  • Recognition at state level that data science is important.
  • Standards start by freshmen year of high school.
C
  • Statistics or some data analysis in math standards; less material or less emphasis on data than B states.
  • State focus is partially there, potentially on computer science and so indirectly on data science.
  • Elective math classes could have a data science component.
  • State may have also be very focused on data science, but in early planning or piloting stages.
D
  • Some data analysis in math, but state retains emphasis on traditional mathematics pathway.
  • Computer science may have data analysis, but not widely taken.
  • Has more detailed statistics standards than an F state, and instruction in statistics could extend to middle school.
F
  • Essentially no state interest or initiative on data science education.
  • Emphasis on traditional math pathways and classes.
  • May have a small amount of statistics in math standards.

Why did we do this?

Our modern world is filled with data.

Understanding and interpreting it is essential to navigating the 21st century.

90% of all the world’s data was created in the last two years.
Every second, humans create enough data to fill 50 Libraries of Congress.

Employers agree.

The number of data scientist roles have grown 650% since 2012.
Many of the fastest growing jobs are data centric.

Students need data science education for the modern world, but schools aren’t teaching it.

Only 10% of high school students take a statistics course.
And, statistics only partially covers the necessary elements of data science.

US states play a vital role in promoting and spreading data science education.

State-mandated standards and graduation requirements determine K-12 curricula.
But we wondered, how many of those standards incorporate data science?
